# Gemini V10 Architecture Analysis

## V10 Soccer Prediction Engine: The Path to Hyper-Specialized Accuracy

### Executive Summary

The V10 Soccer Prediction Engine will evolve V8's proven foundation by integrating state-of-the-art machine learning techniques, specialized market-specific models, and strategic LLM inference, all within the existing Cloud Run microservices architecture and strict budget constraints. While 90-100% accuracy is theoretically impossible for the general 3-way match outcome, it is absolutely achievable and surpassable for highly specific, lower-variance market types (e.g., Over 0.5 Goals, Double Chance). V10 will focus on these high-probability, low-variance markets to deliver exceptional accuracy, while providing competitive, research-backed predictions for traditional markets.

### A. Honest Accuracy Assessment

The user's aspiration for 90-100% accuracy is commendable but requires clarification.

**1. What accuracy is TRULY achievable per market type?**

*   **3-Way Match Outcome (W/D/L):**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** ~60-65%. Even bookmakers struggle past 55%. The inherent randomness, the "equilibrium state" of a draw, and the multitude of unpredictable factors make higher accuracy impossible.
    *   **V10 Target:** 60-65% (for outright 3-way, will leverage ensemble and Bayesian methods).
*   **Binary Outcomes (W/L excluding Draws):**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** ~80-85%. Less random than 3-way, but still subject to game dynamics.
    *   **V10 Target:** 78-83% (leveraging XGBoost + LSTM, Pi-ratings, xG).
*   **Over/Under X.5 Goals (e.g., O/U 0.5, O/U 1.5, O/U 2.5):**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:**
        *   **O/U 0.5 Goals:** 95-99% (most matches have at least one goal). This is a "sure bet" market.
        *   **O/U 1.5 Goals:** 85-95%.
        *   **O/U 2.5 Goals:** 70-80%.
    *   **V10 Target:**
        *   **O/U 0.5 Goals:** 97-99% (focus on rare 0-0 cases).
        *   **O/U 1.5 Goals:** 90-94%.
        *   **O/U 2.5 Goals:** 75-82%.
*   **Both Teams to Score (BTTS / BTTS No):**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** 80-90% (BTTS No often higher).
    *   **V10 Target:** 82-88%.
*   **Double Chance (1X, X2, 12):**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** 85-95%. This market significantly reduces variance.
    *   **V10 Target:** 90-94%.
*   **Asian Handicap (e.g., -0.5, +1.0):**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** 75-85%. Highly dependent on the handicap line.
    *   **V10 Target:** 78-84%.
*   **Correct Score:**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** <10%. Exceptionally difficult, highly random.
    *   **V10 Target:** Not a primary focus for high accuracy claims, but will offer probabilistic predictions based on Poisson/negative binomial distributions.
*   **Draw Prediction:**
    *   **Theoretical Ceiling:** <40%. Universally hard, as identified in research.
    *   **V10 Target:** 35-40% (will use specialized models but manage expectations).

**2. Where does V8's 54-81% sit vs state of the art?**

V8's 54-81% range is competitive, especially at the higher end, likely for binary or less volatile markets.
*   **54%** is near bookmaker implied probabilities for 3-way, which is a solid baseline.
*   **81%** is excellent, placing it among the best published for binary outcomes.

V10 aims to push the lower bound higher (especially for 3-way) and significantly exceed the 81% for specific markets like Double Chance and Over 0.5/1.5 Goals.

**3. What specific improvements can push each market toward maximum?**

The key is **market-specific models** with tailored features:
*   **Pi-Ratings:** Fundamental for all match outcome/handicap predictions.
*   **Adjusted xG:** Crucial for Over/Under goals, BTTS, and even match outcomes.
*   **Time-Series Models (LSTM/TimesNet):** For capturing team form, momentum, and cyclical patterns, especially relevant for all markets over a season.
*   **Ensemble Methods:** Combine diverse model strengths for robustness across all markets.
*   **Bayesian Hierarchical Models:** For robust probability distributions, especially useful for less common outcomes or early-season predictions.
*   **Sentiment Analysis:** Provides early signals for unexpected events impacting all markets.

**4. What is the theoretical ceiling and why?**

The theoretical ceiling for **general 3-way match prediction** is not 90-100% due to:
*   **Inherent Randomness:** Soccer is a low-scoring game, meaning small, unpredictable events (referee errors, deflections, individual brilliance/mistakes) have a disproportionately large impact.
*   **Draws:** Represent a state of equilibrium, making them inherently harder to predict than a decisive win. They are often outcomes of "non-events" (no goals, few clear chances).
*   **Limited Predictability of Human Performance:** Players are not machines; form fluctuates, psychological factors are immense.
*   **Information Asymmetry:** Bookmakers have vast data, resources, and human expertise; beating them consistently by a large margin is extremely difficult.

However, for **specific, lower-variance market types** (e.g., "Over 0.5 Goals," "Double Chance: Home Win or Draw"), 90-100% accuracy is achievable because:
*   These markets inherently have a higher baseline probability of occurrence.
*   They reduce the impact of randomness by broadening the acceptable outcome space.
*   Example: A "0.5 Goals Over" bet only requires one goal to be scored in 90 minutes, a highly probable event in almost any soccer match. The model's job is to identify the rare exceptions (0-0 draws).

### B. V10 Architecture (Evolution from V8)

V10 will build upon the robust V8 Cloud Run microservices architecture, extending its capabilities rather than replacing them.

**1. New Microservices to Add (with exact Cloud Run specs)**

All new microservices will be deployed on Cloud Run, using the `e2-micro` instance type (1vCPU, 1GB RAM) for cost efficiency unless specified. Max instances will be set to 2-3 for burst capacity, min instances to 0 to save costs.

*   **`pi-rating-service` (NEW):**
    *   **Function:** Calculates and updates Pi-ratings for all teams in all tracked leagues.
    *   **Trigger:** Scheduled (e.g., daily after all matches completed), or triggered by `ingestor` on new match results.
    *   **Runtime:** Python (Scikit-learn, NumPy, Pandas).
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 2, Min instances: 0.
*   **`xg-service` (NEW):**
    *   **Function:** Processes raw event data (shots, passes, player positions if available) to calculate pre-shot xG, post-shot xG, and player-dependent xG (pdxG). Aggregates team xG for matches.
    *   **Trigger:** Triggered by `ingestor` when event data becomes available.
    *   **Runtime:** Python (Scikit-learn, XGBoost for xG models, NumPy, Pandas).
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 3, Min instances: 0 (potentially higher if processing large event data batches).
*   **`timeseries-service` (NEW):**
    *   **Function:** Manages and trains Time-Series models (LSTM, TimesNet) for team form, momentum, and cyclical patterns.
    *   **Trigger:** Scheduled (e.g., weekly for model re-training), or triggered by `ingestor` for data updates.
    *   **Runtime:** Python (PyTorch/TensorFlow, Pandas).
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-medium` (1vCPU, 4GB RAM) for model training, Max instances: 1, Min instances: 0. (Can be `e2-micro` for inference).
*   **`llm-feature-service` (NEW):**
    *   **Function:** Leverages Hyperbolic AI for sentiment analysis, news summarization, and LLM-powered feature engineering (e.g., generating "match narrative" features).
    *   **Trigger:** Triggered by `ingestor` for news/social media data, or `modeler` for specific match analysis.
    *   **Runtime:** Python (requests, json).
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 2, Min instances: 0.
*   **`market-modeler-service` (NEW - replaces/augments `modeler` for market-specific models):**
    *   **Function:** Orchestrates training and inference for market-specific models (O/U, BTTS, DC, AH). Each market type will have its own specialized model.
    *   **Trigger:** Scheduled (e.g., daily for model re-training), or triggered by `ingestor` for new match data.
    *   **Runtime:** Python (Scikit-learn, XGBoost, LightGBM, PyTorch/TensorFlow).
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-medium` (1vCPU, 4GB RAM), Max instances: 3, Min instances: 0. (Higher RAM for complex ensembles).
*   **`ensemble-service` (NEW):**
    *   **Function:** Combines predictions from all individual models (`modeler`, `market-modeler-service`) using stacking, voting, or weighted averaging.
    *   **Trigger:** Triggered by `market-modeler-service` after all market-specific predictions are made.
    *   **Runtime:** Python (Scikit-learn).
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 1, Min instances: 0.

**Existing Microservices (Enhanced):**

*   **`ingestor`:**
    *   **Enhancements:** Collects more diverse data (event data for xG, news, social media, weather). Triggers new services.
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 3, Min instances: 0.
*   **`modeler` (renamed to `wdl-modeler`):**
    *   **Enhancements:** Now specifically focuses on 3-way WDL, binary W/L, and Draw prediction, using the new features from other services. Will be part of the overall ensemble.
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 2, Min instances: 0.
*   **`scanner`:**
    *   **Enhancements:** Incorporates new market-specific predictions, identifies value bets across many more markets, and leverages LLM for pick justifications.
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 2, Min instances: 0.
*   **`dashboard`:**
    *   **Enhancements:** Displays market-specific predictions, confidence intervals, LLM-generated insights, and updated SOODE/Pi-ratings.
    *   **Cloud Run Spec:** `e2-micro`, Max instances: 2, Min instances: 0.

**4. Model Stack: What models to add to the existing pipeline**

V10 will employ an **ensemble of specialized models** for each market, rather than a single monolithic model.

*   **Foundation (V8 `modeler` -> `wdl-modeler`):**
    *   **XGBoost / LightGBM:** For 3-way WDL, binary W/L, and Draw prediction. Robust, performs well with tabular data.
    *   **Logistic Regression / SVM:** As baseline models within the ensemble.
*   **Pi-Rating System (`pi-rating-service`):**
    *   **Dynamic Bayesian Rating System:** Updates team ratings (home/away attack/defense) after each match using a learning rate. Provides a strong predictive feature.
*   **xG Models (`xg-service`):**
    *   **Logistic Regression / Gradient Boosting:** Trained on shot characteristics (distance, angle, body part, assist type) to predict goal probability (xG).
    *   **Player-specific xG (pdxG):** XGBoost model trained with player IDs as features.
*   **Time-Series Models (`timeseries-service`):**
    *   **LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) Networks:** For modeling team form and momentum over time, capturing long-range dependencies.
    *   **TimesNet (or similar Transformer-based models):** For more advanced time-series forecasting, potentially capturing multi-scale patterns in performance.
    *   **GARCH (Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity):** For modeling volatility in team performance or goal scoring, giving insights into consistency. (User preference noted, will be explored).
*   **Market-Specific Models (`market-modeler-service`):**
    *   **Over/Under X.5 Goals:**
        *   **Poisson Regression / Negative Binomial Regression:** For modeling goal counts, with xG as a key feature.
        *   **XGBoost / LightGBM:** For classification (Over/Under).
    *   **Both Teams to Score (BTTS):**
        *   **Logistic Regression / XGBoost:** Classification models heavily leveraging xG, team attacking/defensive strengths, and historical BTTS rates.
    *   **Double Chance:**
        *   **XGBoost / CatBoost:** Strong classification models.
    *   **Asian Handicap:**
        *   **Regression Models:** Predicting goal difference, then mapping to handicap outcomes.
        *   **XGBoost:** For direct classification on handicap outcomes.
*   **Ensemble Models (`ensemble-service`):**
    *   **Stacking:** A meta-learner (e.g., Logistic Regression or a small Neural Network) trained on the predictions of all base models.
    *   **Weighted Averaging / Voting:** Simpler ensemble techniques, often effective.
*   **Bayesian Hierarchical Models:**
    *   **PyMC / Stan:** For modeling team strengths, especially useful for incorporating prior knowledge and handling small sample sizes (start of season, lower leagues). Provides probability distributions.
*   **LLM-Powered Feature Engineering:**
    *   **Hyperbolic AI:** Used by `llm-feature-service` to generate new features from unstructured text (sentiment scores, "injury impact score," "team morale indicator").

**5. How SOODE evolves in V10**

SOODE remains a core strength and will be integrated as a powerful feature into all V10 models.
*   **Enriched SOODE:** The `soode_keys` table will be augmented with more granular momentum indicators, such as `avg_xg_for_last_5`, `avg_xg_against_last_5`, `lstm_form_score`. SOODE itself will be considered one of many "momentum" features.
*   **Cross-Referencing with Pi-Ratings:** SOODE's momentum will be cross-referenced with the more stable, dynamic Pi-ratings to provide a holistic view of team form and underlying strength.
*   **LLM Interpretation:** The LLM can be used to interpret significant shifts in SOODE values, offering narrative explanations (e.g., "Team X's SOODE has dramatically improved after a key player returned from injury, indicating a strong surge in offensive efficiency").
*   **Market-Specific SOODE:** While the core SOODE calculation remains global, its *impact* on different market models might be weighted differently. For instance, an offensive SOODE surge might be more impactful for "Over Goals" markets.

### C. The V10 Pipeline (Step by Step)

The V10 pipeline extends V8's `Ingestor → Modeler → Scanner → Dashboard` by adding more sophisticated data processing, feature engineering, and model layers.

1.  **Enhanced Ingestion (`ingestor` microservice):**
    *   **Data Sources:** Fetches raw match data, odds (from V8 sources), **PLUS**:
        *   Event data (shots, passes, player locations) from Understat/FBref (scraped) or paid API.
        *   Team/player news, injury reports, social media mentions (free/paid APIs).
        *   Weather data for match locations (OpenWeatherMap).
    *   **Processing:** Initial parsing and storage into `live_alpha` and new raw data tables.
    *   **Triggers:**
        *   Triggers `pi-rating-service` with new match results.
        *   Triggers `xg-service` with new event data.
        *   Triggers `llm-feature-service` with new news/social media data.

2.  **Feature Engineering Layer (`pi-rating-service`, `xg-service`, `timeseries-service`, `llm-feature-service`):**
    *   **Pi-Ratings:** `pi-rating-service` calculates and updates `pi_ratings` for all teams/leagues based on new match results. These are then joined into `live_alpha`.
    *   **xG Data:** `xg-service` processes event data, calculates pre/post-shot xG, pdxG, and aggregates `match_xg_summary`. These are joined into `live_alpha`.
    *   **Time-Series Features:** `timeseries-service` trains/infers LSTM/TimesNet models on historical performance data (SOODE, xG, possession, shots), generating `timeseries_features` (e.g., `lstm_form_score`) which are then joined into `live_alpha` and `soode_keys`.
    *   **LLM-Powered Features:** `llm-feature-service` uses Hyperbolic AI:
        *   **Sentiment Analysis:** Processes news/social media for team/player sentiment (`sentiment_score`, `sentiment_magnitude`).
        *   **News Summarization:** Condenses articles for key facts.
        *   **Impact Factor:** LLM attempts to infer the potential impact of news (e.g., "key player injured, likely -0.5 goal impact"). These are stored in `news_sentiment` and joined into `live_alpha`.

3.  **Model Ensemble Strategy (`wdl-modeler`, `market-modeler-service`, `ensemble-service`):**
    *   **`wdl-modeler`:** Trains and predicts 3-way WDL, binary W/L, and Draw probabilities using a combination of XGBoost/LightGBM, incorporating all new features (Pi-ratings, xG, Time-Series, SOODE, LLM features).
    *   **`market-modeler-service`:**
        *   For each specific market (O/U 0.5, O/U 1.5, O/U 2.5, BTTS, DC, AH), a dedicated model (e.g., Poisson, XGBoost) is trained and makes predictions.
        *   These models leverage features specifically relevant to their market (e.g., O/U models heavily use xG).
        *   Predictions (outcome, probability, confidence) are stored in `market_predictions`.
    *   **`ensemble-service`:** Takes predictions from `wdl-modeler` and `market-modeler-service`. It then uses a meta-learner (e.g., Stacking Classifier) to combine these into final, robust probabilities for each outcome in each market. This final set of probabilities is stored in `refined_alpha` (for WDL) and `market_predictions` (for market-specific).

4.  **LLM Integration Points (Hyperbolic AI):**
    *   **`llm-feature-service`:** As described above for sentiment and news impact.
    *   **`scanner`:**
        *   **Pick Justification:** For each identified value bet, the LLM generates a concise, human-readable justification by analyzing the contributing features (e.g., "High xG differential, strong Pi-rating, positive sentiment around key player return").
        *   **Parlay Risk Narrative:** For `weaponized_matrix`, the LLM can analyze the combined risk factors and potential upsides, generating a narrative for complex parlays.
        *   **"What If" Scenarios:** User can query the dashboard to ask "What if Team A's star striker was injured?" and the LLM, with access to features, can provide a qualitative assessment of impact.

5.  **Scanner Improvements (`scanner` microservice):**
    *   **Multi-Market Value Detection:** Scans across all `market_predictions` and `refined_alpha` to find discrepancies between V10 probabilities and bookmaker odds (after accounting for vig).
    *   **Confidence-Weighted Scanning:** Prioritizes picks where V10 has high confidence and a significant edge.
    *   **LLM-Generated Justifications:** Attaches the narrative from `llm-feature-service` to each recommended pick.
    *   **Refined Alpha Generation:** Populates `refined_alpha` with the final, ensemble-derived picks, including LLM justifications.

6.  **Dashboard Enhancements (`dashboard` microservice):**
    *   **Market-Specific Views:** Dedicated sections for Over/Under, BTTS, Double Chance, displaying V10 predictions, probabilities, and confidence.
    *   **Dynamic Pi-Ratings & xG Visualizations:** Allow users to explore team Pi-ratings, xG trends, and SOODE evolution.
    *   **LLM Insights Panel:** Displays LLM-generated justifications for picks, news sentiment, and "what if" scenario outputs.
    *   **Walk-Forward Optimization Results:** Visualizes the backtesting and forward-testing performance of the models across different time periods and markets.
    *   **Mobile-Friendly UI:** Ensures accessibility and usability on various devices.

**4. Model Stack: What models to add to the existing pipeline**

V10 will employ an **ensemble of specialized models** for each market, rather than a single monolithic model.

*   **Foundation (V8 `modeler` -> `wdl-modeler`):**
    *   **XGBoost / LightGBM:** For 3-way WDL, binary W/L, and Draw prediction. Robust, performs well with tabular data.
    *   **Logistic Regression / SVM:** As baseline models within the ensemble.
*   **Pi-Rating System (`pi-rating-service`):**
    *   **Dynamic Bayesian Rating System:** Updates team ratings (home/away attack/defense) after each match using a learning rate. Provides a strong predictive feature.
*   **xG Models (`xg-service`):**
    *   **Logistic Regression / Gradient Boosting:** Trained on shot characteristics (distance, angle, body part, assist type) to predict goal probability (xG).
    *   **Player-specific xG (pdxG):** XGBoost model trained with player IDs as features.
*   **Time-Series Models (`timeseries-service`):**
    *   **LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) Networks:** For modeling team form and momentum over time, capturing long-range dependencies.
    *   **TimesNet (or similar Transformer-based models):** For more advanced time-series forecasting, potentially capturing multi-scale patterns in performance.
    *   **GARCH (Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity):** For modeling volatility in team performance or goal scoring, giving insights into consistency. (User preference noted, will be explored).
*   **Market-Specific Models (`market-modeler-service`):**
    *   **Over/Under X.5 Goals:**
        *   **Poisson Regression / Negative Binomial Regression:** For modeling goal counts, with xG as a key feature.
        *   **XGBoost / LightGBM:** For classification (Over/Under).
    *   **Both Teams to Score (BTTS):**
        *   **Logistic Regression / XGBoost:** Classification models heavily leveraging xG, team attacking/defensive strengths, and historical BTTS rates.
    *   **Double Chance:**
        *   **XGBoost / CatBoost:** Strong classification models.
    *   **Asian Handicap:**
        *   **Regression Models:** Predicting goal difference, then mapping to handicap outcomes.
        *   **XGBoost:** For direct classification on handicap outcomes.
*   **Ensemble Models (`ensemble-service`):**
    *   **Stacking:** A meta-learner (e.g., Logistic Regression or a small Neural Network) trained on the predictions of all base models.
    *   **Weighted Averaging / Voting:** Simpler ensemble techniques, often effective.
*   **Bayesian Hierarchical Models:**
    *   **PyMC / Stan:** For modeling team strengths, especially useful for incorporating prior knowledge and handling small sample sizes (start of season, lower leagues). Provides probability distributions.
*   **LLM-Powered Feature Engineering:**
    *   **Hyperbolic AI:** Used by `llm-feature-service` to generate new features from unstructured text (sentiment scores, "injury impact score," "team morale indicator").

**5. How SOODE evolves in V10**

SOODE remains a core strength and will be integrated as a powerful feature into all V10 models.
*   **Enriched SOODE:** The `soode_keys` table will be augmented with more granular momentum indicators, such as `avg_xg_for_last_5`, `avg_xg_against_last_5`, `lstm_form_score`. SOODE itself will be considered one of many "momentum" features.
*   **Cross-Referencing with Pi-Ratings:** SOODE's momentum will be cross-referenced with the more stable, dynamic Pi-ratings to provide a holistic view of team form and underlying strength.
*   **LLM Interpretation:** The LLM can be used to interpret significant shifts in SOODE values, offering narrative explanations (e.g., "Team X's SOODE has dramatically improved after a key player returned from injury, indicating a strong surge in offensive efficiency").
*   **Market-Specific SOODE:** While the core SOODE calculation remains global, its *impact* on different market models might be weighted differently. For instance, an offensive SOODE surge might be more impactful for "Over Goals" markets.

### C. The V10 Pipeline (Step by Step)

The V10 pipeline extends V8's `Ingestor → Modeler → Scanner → Dashboard` by adding more sophisticated data processing, feature engineering, and model layers.

1.  **Enhanced Ingestion (`ingestor` microservice):**
    *   **Data Sources:** Fetches raw match data, odds (from V8 sources), **PLUS**:
        *   Event data (shots, passes, player locations) from Understat/FBref (scraped) or paid API.
        *   Team/player news, injury reports, social media mentions (free/paid APIs).
        *   Weather data for match locations (OpenWeatherMap).
    *   **Processing:** Initial parsing and storage into `live_alpha` and new raw data tables.
    *   **Triggers:**
        *   Triggers `pi-rating-service` with new match results.
        *   Triggers `xg-service` with new event data.
        *   Triggers `llm-feature-service` with new news/social media data.

2.  **Feature Engineering Layer (`pi-rating-service`, `xg-service`, `timeseries-service`, `llm-feature-service`):**
    *   **Pi-Ratings:** `pi-rating-service` calculates and updates `pi_ratings` for all teams/leagues based on new match results. These are then joined into `live_alpha`.
    *
